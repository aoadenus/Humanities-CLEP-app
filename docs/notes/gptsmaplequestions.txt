# Option 3 — Sample-Section Study Help

The official guide says its sample questions are there to show the **format and difficulty**, not to reveal the live exam. In the visible sample section, the guide includes items about a satirical classic, race and housing in modern drama, a river journey in Africa, the phoenix as a rebirth symbol, the lute’s modern relative, and troubadours. Those examples show the exam is really testing **recognition of major works, themes, symbols, instruments, and cultural roles** — not just raw memorization.  

## What those sample items are really testing

### 1. Satirical novel recognition

You are expected to identify a major work from a short description of tone, plot, or characters.

### 2. Social-theme drama recognition

You may need to identify a play from its central conflict, especially race, class, family, or identity.

### 3. Canonical fiction plot clue

You may get one striking plot marker and need to know the book immediately.

### 4. Mythic/symbolic meaning

The exam can ask what a recurring symbol means across art and literature.

### 5. Instrument family recognition

You need broad recognition of music history and instrument relationships.

### 6. Cultural-role recognition

You should know what groups like troubadours, humanists, monks, or modernists actually did.

## Fast skill labels for those sample themes

* **Work identification**
* **Theme recognition**
* **Symbol recognition**
* **Music terminology**
* **Historical role recognition**
* **Cross-discipline cultural literacy**
