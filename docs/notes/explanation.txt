* full image only when tapped

### Lazy-load everything heavy

* images
* long summaries
* videos
* galleries

### Cache aggressively

Once chapter 2 is downloaded, it should open even offline.

### Queue writes offline

If he finishes a quiz without internet:

* save locally
* sync later

That is non-negotiable for mobile feeling good.

# 9. Offline-first behavior

For this app, offline support is worth it.

## What should work offline

* reading downloaded chapter summaries
* flashcards
* previously downloaded quizzes
* games
* notes
* seeing prior progress

## What can require internet

* new video embeds
* syncing attempts
* downloading new chapters
* cloud backup

## Sync model

The app should:

1. save locally first
2. update UI immediately
3. sync in background
4. resolve conflicts by latest timestamp or server merge rules

This is how mobile apps feel smooth.

## Official Humanities CLEP exam

### What the exam covers

The Humanities CLEP tests **general knowledge of literature, art, music, and the other performing arts**. It is broad across **all periods from classical to contemporary** and includes **poetry, prose, philosophy, art, architecture, music, dance, theater, and film**. The exam expects students to show **specific knowledge, concept understanding, and analysis/interpretation of works of art**.  

### Exam length and size

The exam contains **about 140 questions** to be answered in **90 minutes**. Some of those are **pretest questions** and do **not** count toward the score. 

## Official skill weighting

Questions are designed around these abilities:

* **50%** knowledge of factual information, such as **authors and works**
* **30%** recognition of techniques, style, rhyme scheme, medium, and identification of writers, artists, schools, or periods
* **20%** understanding and interpretation of literary passages and art reproductions that will likely be unfamiliar to most test takers 

## Official subject breakdown

### Literature — **50%**

* **10%** Drama
* **10–15%** Poetry
* **15–20%** Fiction
* **10%** Nonfiction, including philosophy 

### The Arts — **50%**

* **20%** Visual arts: painting, sculpture, etc.
* **5%** Visual arts: architecture
* **15%** Performing arts: music
* **10%** Performing arts: film, dance, etc. 

## Official historical period coverage

The exam says questions are **fairly evenly divided** among these periods:

* Classical
* Medieval and Renaissance
* Seventeenth and eighteenth centuries
* Nineteenth century
* Twentieth and twenty-first centuries 

## Other-cultures requirement

The guide says **at least 5–10%** of the questions draw on **other cultures**, including **African, Asian, and Latin American** material. It also says some questions **cross disciplines and/or chronological periods**, and a substantial number test **terminology, genre, and style**. 

## Important note about images

The guide notes that while some images in the printed guide are black and white, **works shown on the actual exam will be in color**. 

## Important note about “the official exam”

What the guide gives you is the **official exam description and blueprint**, plus **sample questions**. It does **not** publish the actual live exam questions. The guide explicitly says the sample questions are **representative** and **do not appear on any CLEP examination**. 

## The exact app implication

So for your app, the **real official exam spec** to build around is:

* **140 questions**
* **90 minutes**
* **50% literature / 50% arts**
* skills weighted **50 factual / 30 recognition-style / 20 interpretation**
* periods fairly balanced across the five major eras
* **5–10% non-Western / other cultures**
* meaningful coverage of **terminology, genre, and style** 

That is the official backbone. The rest of your course design should hang off this, not off random prep material.
